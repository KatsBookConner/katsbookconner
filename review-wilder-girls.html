Wilder Girls

Rory Power

The rating is...

What I thought...

"Wilder Girls" by Rory Power presents readers with a chilling and atmospheric tale set in an isolated boarding school ravaged by a mysterious disease. The novel follows three teenage girls – Hetty, Byatt, and Reese – as they navigate the dangers of their quarantined island home, uncovering dark secrets and confronting their own fears along the way.

Power excels at creating a sense of unease and tension throughout the narrative. The island setting is vividly rendered, evoking a palpable sense of isolation and dread. The mystery surrounding the Tox – the disease that has transformed the landscape and its inhabitants – adds an intriguing layer of suspense to the story, keeping readers guessing until the very end.

The characters in "Wilder Girls" are compelling and multifaceted, each grappling with their own demons in the face of adversity. Hetty, in particular, is a sympathetic protagonist whose determination to uncover the truth drives much of the plot forward. Power does an excellent job of exploring themes of friendship, loyalty, and survival, as the girls band together to confront the horrors of their reality.

However, despite its many strengths, "Wilder Girls" ultimately falls short in its execution. The pacing can be uneven at times, with certain sections of the novel dragging while others feel rushed. Additionally, the resolution of the central mystery leaves something to be desired, with several plot threads left frustratingly unresolved.

Furthermore, some readers may find the novel's graphic content and dark themes off-putting. Power does not shy away from depicting violence and gore, which may not be suitable for all audiences.

In conclusion, "Wilder Girls" is a dark and compelling thriller that showcases Rory Power's talent for crafting atmospheric and suspenseful narratives. While the novel is not without its flaws – including uneven pacing and a somewhat unsatisfying resolution – it nevertheless offers an engaging read for fans of dystopian fiction and psychological thrillers. With its memorable characters and haunting setting, "Wilder Girls" is sure to leave a lasting impression on readers who dare to venture into its twisted world.
