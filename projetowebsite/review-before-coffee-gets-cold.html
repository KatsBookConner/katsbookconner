Before the Coffee Gets Cold

Toshikazu Kawaguchi

The rating is...

What I thought...

"Before the Coffee Gets Cold" by Toshikazu Kawaguchi invites readers into a quaint café in Tokyo that offers its patrons a unique opportunity – the chance to travel back in time. Through a set of specific rules, customers can revisit moments in their past, but there's a catch: they must return before their coffee gets cold, or they risk being trapped in the past forever.

Kawaguchi's premise is undeniably intriguing, and the novel's exploration of time travel and its consequences is thought-provoking. Each chapter follows a different character as they grapple with their regrets and seek solace or closure in revisiting pivotal moments from their past. From lost loves to unresolved family conflicts, the stories presented in "Before the Coffee Gets Cold" are poignant and relatable, touching on universal themes of love, loss, and forgiveness.

The café itself serves as a charming backdrop for these narratives, with its cozy atmosphere and enigmatic proprietor adding to the book's whimsical appeal. Kawaguchi's prose is simple yet evocative, allowing readers to immerse themselves fully in the world of the café and its patrons.

However, despite its intriguing premise and heartfelt storytelling, "Before the Coffee Gets Cold" falls short in terms of execution. The novel's structure, which consists of interconnected vignettes rather than a cohesive narrative arc, can feel disjointed at times, making it difficult for readers to fully invest in the characters and their stories. Additionally, the rules governing time travel within the café are never fully explained, leaving certain plot points feeling underdeveloped and unsatisfying.

Furthermore, while the novel touches on profound themes of regret and redemption, it ultimately lacks depth in its exploration of these concepts. Characters' motivations and emotional journeys often feel surface-level, with missed opportunities for deeper introspection and growth.

In conclusion, "Before the Coffee Gets Cold" is a charming and heartfelt novel that offers an intriguing take on time travel and its implications. While its premise and storytelling are undeniably engaging, the novel is hindered by its disjointed structure and lack of depth in character development. Nevertheless, readers looking for a light and whimsical read may find enjoyment in Kawaguchi's tale of love, loss, and second chances.
